Digital Collection platform community

Since 2011, many precious collections such as Newton and Charles Darwin have been shared to the work via the Cambridge University Digital Library. The underlying platform, developed by the team at Cambridge University Library, is now available as an open-source project, free to all to use, so that many more collections can be shared for all to study, research or simply enjoy!

Learn more


Dr Jessica Gardner, University Librarian and Director of Library Services.

"Cambridge University Library collections represent an invaluable contribution to world heritage spanning four thousand years of human thought. Cambridge Digital Library seeks to open up this knowledge to the widest possible global audience, for research, for education or for simple curiosity and wonder."

About

The CDCP is an open-source project that underpins the digital collections at Cambridge, Manchester and Lancaster Universities. This community site is dedicated to help adopters of the platform for developers and metadata specialists.
